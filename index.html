RGB Consortium

Industrial consortium maintaining RGB free software project. RGB protocol is the first smart contract system made with client-side validation, which works on top of bitcoin & lightning network and doesn't require any softforks.

Overview

Swiss Association under Articles 60 of Civil Code

Not for-profit

Takes over RGB codebase on RGB-WG GitHub organization & corresponding crates on crates.io

Ossifies RGB consensus

Creates and manages specifications and documentation

Manages RGB standard library and toolchain evolution

Membership

How to become a member

Standard or Steering member

An individual or an organization may become a Standard or Steering member by either:

  • Contributing to RGB development in a significant way

    The contributions must happen under the codebase managed by the Consortium.

  • Sponsoring the Consortium

    (Except for the invited founding members, this will require approval of the Board of Directors.)

Associated member

An organization may become an Associated member by expressing strong intent to integrate or build products with RGB.

Except for the invited associate members, this also requires approval of the Board of Directors.

Budgeting

Income

  • Sponsorship
    • Large: $150k a year per steering member
    • Small: $50k a year per ordinary member
  • Support fees
    • Priority: $25k a year
    • Standard: from $15k a year
  • Ad-hoc fundraising campaigns

Spendings

  • Salary to codebase maintainers (fixed)
  • Security audits (ad-hoc)
  • Toolchain dev grants
  • Legal maintenance and accounting (fixed, ~1–5k a year)
  • Social media marketing (X/Twitter etc)
  • Events sponsorship
  • Legal protection of devs from litigations (case-by-case)

RGB Consensus Maintenance

Based on v0.12 of the protocol, released on the 10th of June

Maintainers are elected and removed by a majority of steering committee

Changes can be merged and published only with 100% consensus of codebase maintainers (with the goal of ossification)

RGB Standard LIB & Toolchain

Includes

  • Standard library and wallet libraries
  • Command-line tools
  • RGB Node
  • Contractum language

New components

  • Developed using grant program by selected third-party companies (selection done by board of directors)
  • Once developed, maintained by RGB Consortium

Maintainers

  • Elected and removed by a majority of general assembly (steering + ordinary members)
  • Changes can be merged and published once agreed by majority of maintainers (with the goal of fast evolution)
